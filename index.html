Soon: FY 2013-15 Adopted Budget Visualized; New Tools and Policy Initiatives; Usability Testing

We expect the budget office to publish data for the FY 2013-15 Adopted Budget in late July early August. Follow us or join our mailing list (below) to stay informed.

In the meantime, based on learnings from the recent budget process, we're developing tools and policy initiatives to enable greater understanding and participation. We need your help!

Join us on Tuesdays, 6:30 p.m. at City Hall, hearing room 3. We need everyone: community advocates, coders, journalists, city staff. And if you only have 30 minutes, and like home-made cookies, please take our usability test.

Beyond the 2-Year Budget: Deficits

Oakland's current budget debate has focused largely on the next two years, in which the city's budget is balanced. Less discussed is what happens in the years after. According to the city's five-year forecast, we can expect significant deficits beginning in FY 2015-16.

How should Oakland's short-term budget decisions be based on our long-term financial health? Discuss.
